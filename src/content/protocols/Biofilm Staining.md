
### Materials

- **M63 medium**
- **0.1% crystal violet solution**
- **30% acetic acid aqueous solution**
- **AiiA protein solution**
  - AiiA protein dissolution buffer (10 mM Tris-HCl, 1 mM EDTA, 6% trehalose, pH 8.0)
  - AiiA protein dissolution solution (1 μg/μL)
  - AiiA protein working stock solution (20 μg/mL)

### Procedure

#### 1. Inoculum Preparation

- Inoculate overnight cultures of Pseudomonas aeruginosa in TSB at a 1:50 ratio into fresh M63 medium. Inoculate Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Saccharomyces boulardii at a 1:20 ratio into M63 medium. For yeast, centrifuge at 5,000 × g for 1 minute, remove the supernatant, and resuspend in an equal volume of M63 medium. Repeat this washing step three times in total.

#### 2. Time-Course Biofilm Growth

- Take a pre-sterilized 96-well plate and add 50 μL of diluted Pseudomonas aeruginosa culture to wells A1-A6, B1-B6, and C1-C6.
- Add 50 μL of sterile water to wells A1-A6, and mix by pipetting.
- Add 50 μL of diluted Saccharomyces cerevisiae culture to wells B1-B6, and mix by pipetting.
- Add 50 μL of diluted Saccharomyces boulardii culture to wells C1-C6, and mix by pipetting.
- Seal the 96-well plate with Parafilm in a biosafety cabinet and incubate at 37°C for the corresponding time points (4h, 8h, 12h, 16h, 20h, 24h).

#### 3. Biofilm Staining and Washing

- Invert the 96-well plate and gently tap to remove the liquid.
- Use the lid of a large pipette tip box or another container as a washing basin. Rinse it thoroughly and fill it with an appropriate amount of ultrapure water. Slowly immerse the 96-well plate into the water, shake gently, remove, and tap to discard the water. Repeat the washing step once more (change the water for the second wash).
- Add 125 μL of 0.1% crystal violet solution to each well and incubate at room temperature for 15 minutes.
- Wash the plate four times, then tap it thoroughly dry on a stack of paper towels (tap firmly).
- Place the plate upside down to air dry (for several hours to overnight, until completely dry). After drying, take a photograph of the plate.
- Dissolve and quantify the crystal violet:
  - Add 125 μL of 30% acetic acid aqueous solution to each well and incubate at room temperature for 15 minutes.
  - Transfer 125 μL of the dissolved crystal violet-acetic acid solution to a new 96-well plate (to allow for margin, uniformly take 100 μL of the solution and convert the absorbance proportionally based on volume).
  - Add 100 μL of 30% acetic acid solution to four wells as control wells, and measure the absorbance at 550 nm.
