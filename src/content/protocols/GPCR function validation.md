### Procedure

**Note:** Maintain sterile conditions throughout the experiment.  
While the biosafety cabinet is under UV sterilization, microplate reader settings can be pre-adjusted.

#### 1. Preparation of Histamine Gradient

- Label histamine gradient solutions from high to low concentration as 4, 3, 2, and 1; PBS buffer serves as solution 0.  

#### 2. Preparation of dATP Gradient

- Mix 8 μL of 100 mM dATP stock solution with 92 μL of PBS; label as solution 3.  
- Dilute 50 μL of solution 3 twofold with PBS; label as solution 2.  
- Dilute 50 μL of solution 2 twofold with PBS; label as solution 1.  
- PBS buffer serves as solution 0.  

**Note:** Store the remaining dATP stock solution at −20 °C after use.

#### 3. Yeast Culture and Sample Addition

- *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* strains were pre-cultured overnight in SD medium  
- Gently resuspend cells before use.  
- Add 140 μL of yeast suspension and 10 μL of the designated reagent to each well according to the layout.

#### 4. Fluorescence Detection

- Add 200 μL of each protein sample to the 96-well plate, and add 200 μL of PBS to another well as a control.
- Detect the samples using the microplate reader’s default wavelengths (excitation wavelength 488 nm, emission wavelength 528 nm).
- Use the fluorescence intensity data provided by the microplate reader to determine the EGFP content in the protein samples, thereby inferring the expression of the exogenous plasmid in the cells.
